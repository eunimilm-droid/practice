Policies

Guidelines on the New Learning Environment Under the New Normal


1. The Library Services Unit considers upgrading the library collection by acquiring e-resources (ebooks and ejournals) and make them available online through the URS Library Portal which students and faculty can access anytime.

2. Online Library Instruction and Virtual Assistance will be provided to answer reference queries.

3. Printed library resources are strictly for room use only. Borrowing privileges are suspended.

4. Book quarantine will be practiced.

5. Library users are required to secure an appointment with the library staff to ensure the implementation of physical distancing. 1 library user for every 1.5 square meters shall be adopted.

6. Information dissemination using social media platforms and the URS Library website will be initiated.

7. The use of equipment in the Electronic Resource Center will be limited. Wireless connectivity is highly encouraged only for academic use.

8. Online access to the library collection will be made available through the URS Online Public Access Catalog.

9. Bibliographic information of theses and dissertations will be uploaded in URS Online Public Access Catalog. The full text will be available upon request considering copyright restrictions.

10. Information dissemination using social media platforms and the URS Library website will be initiated.

11. Health and safety protocols will strictly be observed upon entry to the library. Bringing personal hygiene kits is encouraged.

12. The faculty should provide the library with Instructional Materials granting permission to have it uploaded online considering copyright restrictions.

13. Remote Reference Service and Online Document Delivery Service will be made available to library users in need of reference materials available at the librarySY

Library Rules and Regulations


1. The library is open from 7:00 am to 6:00 pm Mondays thru Fridays and 8:30 am – 5:30 pm on Saturdays.

2. Books are arranged on shelves according to the Library of Congress Classification Scheme.

3. The following are authorized to use the Library.
3.1 Graduate School.
3.2 URS Faculty, administrators and employees.
3.3 Visiting users with referral letters.

4. Borrower’s card is required.

5. Reference books, Periodicals, thesis and dissertation are restricted for room use.

6. Materials returned late are subject to overdue fines.
6.1 Php 8.00/day
6.2 1.00/first hour
6.3 1.00/succeeding hours.

7. Borrowers with overdue books or with standing obligations to the library will not be allowed to borrow unless all library accounts are settled.

8. Users caught bringing out the library materials without permission and/or using others’ library card shall be dealt with appropriate disciplinary action.

9. Library patrons are reminded that the library is primarily for serious study and research, eating, sleeping, littering, loud discussions and the like are not permitted.

10. Silence is strictly observed inside the library.

Guidelines to use Multimedia Center (AVR)


1. The URS Library Audio Visual Center is exclusive to URS Faculty, Students and Staff use.

2. Use is subject to AV Center hours and availability.

3. Reservation shall be made a week before the viewing date.

4. Audio Visual room can only accommodate 15-20 individuals, for large group viewing, the use of Campus Audio-Visual Room is encouraged.

5. The use of the Audio Visual Center is for educational purposes only. Viewing of pornographic films is not allowed.

6. Audio-Visual materials are for library/AVR use only. Materials must be returned right after the viewing period.

7. Duplication of any Audio-Visual material is strictly prohibited.

8. Equipment malfunction should be reported immediately to the person in-charge

9. Proper decorum should be observed at all times.

10. Eating, drinking and sleeping are prohibited.

Guidelines on E-Learning Center Guidelines on E-learning Center


1. The E-Learning Center is open from Monday to Friday, 8:00am to 5:00pm.

2. Reservation is on a first-come first-served basis.

3. Each bonafide user may use a slot for a maximum period of one-hour. He/she may renew his/her reservation for another hour (or a fraction of it) but will have to vacate the slot as soon as another user comes to avail of the same.

4. Upon registration, present Library ID and current registration card with picture.

5. Observe silence and courtesy during stay at the center; headphones are provided for personalized playing of multimedia materials.

6. Bags or personal belongings should be left at the counter provided for such purpose.

7. Viewing of pornographic materials and playing of video games are not allowed.

8. Use of pirated software as well as USB and other similar devices is strictly prohibited.

9. Bringing of foods and drinks inside the center is not allowed;

10. Sharing the computer unit of others and other paraphernalia assigned to him/her is not allowed.

11. Any loss or damage to any equipment/facility used inside the center shall be the responsibility of the user.

12. The URS Administration reserves the right to suspend or cancel the use of facilities if any of the policies has been violated.

13. The University of Rizal System may implement additional rules as it may deem necessary.

14. In case of problem or technical error in the use of computers, request the assistance of the library IT in –Charge for proper handling.

15. Always log-out after navigating the services or before leaving the E-Learning terminal.

Guidelines on Borrowing of Library Resources for Faculty and Staff


1. To avail of the Library Services, an employee must first enroll in the database with the assistance of Library Personnel. The Campus Librarian/Library-in-Charge must coordinate to their Campus Human Resource Management Officer to determine if the employee-borrower is a bonafide employee of the University.

2. A faculty/staff member who wants to borrow a book either for photocopying, classroom use, overnight loan or home reading should borrow the material personally at the library subject to the specific guidelines applicable to the different modes of borrowing. He/She should sign the book card and present it to the Campus Librarian/Library-in-Charge.

3. A maximum of three (3) non-reserves or not in demand books can be borrowed for a week. Failure to return the books on the due date will mean a fine of P20.00 per day per book.

4. Only one (1) book from the reserve collection can be borrowed at a time. For overnight use, the book should be borrowed from 4:00 pm to 5:00 pm and it should be returned before 9:00 am on the following day. Failure to abide by this rule would mean a fine of Php 20.00 per hour; computation of fines will be based on the office hours of the library. Reserved books are allowed to be borrowed for overnight use from Monday to Thursday only.

5. Theses, special projects, dissertations, journals, magazines, newspapers, clippings, non-book materials, electronic resources (e.g. books, journals, maps, portable document format, flash media, audio, moving images, videos, still images installed/saved/formatted in CDs, DVDs, external hard drive, and other similar portable devices that require the use of computers and compatible equipment) and reference books such as encyclopedias, yearbooks, dictionaries, almanacs, atlases, gazetteers, government publications, bibliographies, , directories and indexes are strictly for library use only.

6. Posters, maps, globes may be borrowed for classroom use and should be returned on the same day on the same condition. A fine of Php 20.00 per day per material will be charged for violation of this guideline.

7. Lost, misplaced or damaged books and other reference materials should be reported immediately to the librarian to prevent fines, but must be replaced with the same title within one week. However, if the material to be replaced is no longer available within the allowable period, then it can be replaced with an updated edition, and subjected for approval by the Campus Librarian/Library-in-Charge.

8. When availing the intra-library loan services, campus librarian of the home campus of the borrower must be notified first as to the current status of his/her circulation account. All borrowed library resources from other campus libraries will be entered under his record in the home branch and in the other campus libraries as well. The item borrowed will be put under the rotating collection and additional notes shall be made on the item record. Further, the item will be tagged as transferred to the home library of the borrower. If he/she has already borrowed the maximum number of library resources, he/she will be notified to return library resources in excess in his/her home campus on the following working day. When returning the library resource, the library customer has the option to return it in his/her home campus or to return it to the campus where he/she borrowed the item.
